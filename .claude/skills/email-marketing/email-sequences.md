# Email Sequence Types

## Welcome Series (Days 0-14)

**Purpose:** Onboard new subscribers, establish brand voice, drive first conversion

**Structure:**
| Email | Timing | Goal | Content Type |
|-------|--------|------|--------------|
| 1 | Day 0 (immediate) | Set expectations | Welcome + brand story |
| 2 | Day 3 | Educate | Quick win / tutorial |
| 3 | Day 7 | Build trust | Feature highlight + social proof |
| 4 | Day 14 | Convert | Upgrade/complete profile nudge |

**Best practices:**

- Email 1 should arrive within 5 minutes of signup (expectation is highest)
- Include a clear "What's next?" section in every email
- Use first name personalization from Email 1
- Set email frequency expectations upfront

**Example welcome series:**

```
Email 1: "Welcome to [Brand]! Here's what happens next"
Email 2: "Your first [quick win] in 2 minutes"
Email 3: "How [customer name] achieved [specific result]"
Email 4: "Ready to unlock [premium feature]?"
```

## Nurture Campaigns (Ongoing)

**Purpose:** Keep engaged users active, educate on features, prevent churn

**Types:**

### Educational Drip

- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Content:** Tips, tutorials, use cases
- **Goal:** Increase feature adoption
- **Example:** "5 Ways to Use [Feature] You Probably Didn't Know"

### Product Updates

- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly or monthly
- **Content:** New features, improvements, roadmap
- **Goal:** Show progress, reduce perceived stagnation
- **Example:** "What's New This Month: [3 Features You Asked For]"

### Case Studies / Social Proof

- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Content:** Customer success stories, testimonials
- **Goal:** Build trust, show ROI
- **Example:** "How [Company] Saved 20 Hours/Week with [Product]"

## Re-engagement Sequences (14-30 Days Inactive)

**Purpose:** Win back users who've stopped engaging before they fully churn

**Structure:**
| Email | Timing | Angle | CTA |
|-------|--------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Day 14 | Value reminder | "Here's what you're missing" |
| 2 | Day 21 | Incentive | "Come back -- 20% off for you" |
| 3 | Day 30 | Last chance | "We'll miss you -- one final offer" |

**Best practices:**

- Use "We miss you" tone (emotional, personal)
- Highlight new features since they left
- Offer incentive (discount, free month, bonus feature)
- Include easy opt-out to clean list

**Subject line examples:**

- "We miss you, [First Name]"
- "Still interested in [benefit they signed up for]?"
- "One last thing before you go..."

## Reactivation Campaigns (30+ Days Inactive)

**Purpose:** Last-ditch effort to recover churned users

**Structure:**
| Email | Timing | Strategy | Offer |
|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Day 30 | Feedback request | Survey: "Why did you leave?" |
| 2 | Day 45 | Win-back incentive | 50% off, extended trial, etc. |
| 3 | Day 60 | Final goodbye | Unsubscribe confirmation |

**Best practices:**

- Acknowledge the lapse honestly
- Ask for feedback (survey with incentive)
- Offer irresistible incentive (50% off, not 10%)
- Make unsubscribe easy (clean your list)

**Example:**

```
Subject: "Honestly, we messed up"
Body: "We noticed you haven't used [Product] in a while. Was it us? Let us know in this 2-minute survey, and we'll send you [incentive]."
```

## Transactional Emails

**Purpose:** Deliver expected information (receipts, confirmations, password resets)

**Types:**

### Confirmation Emails

- Account creation
- Order confirmation
- Subscription renewal
- Cancellation confirmation

### Notification Emails

- Password reset
- Security alerts
- Shipping updates
- Payment failures

**Best practices:**

- Send immediately (within 1 minute)
- Clear subject line (no cleverness)
- Include all relevant details (order number, date, amount)
- Provide support contact prominently
- Use transactional sender (no-reply or support@)

**Subject line format:**

```
"Your [Product] account is ready"
"Order #12345 confirmed"
"Action required: Update your payment method"
```
