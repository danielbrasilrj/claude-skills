# Local SEO Reference Guide

## Google Business Profile (GBP) Deep Dive

### Why GBP Matters

Google Business Profile is the single most important local SEO asset. It determines:
- **Local Pack rankings** (top 3 map results in search)
- **Maps visibility** (searches on Google Maps)
- **Knowledge Panel** (right sidebar on branded searches)

**Statistics:**
- 46% of all Google searches have local intent
- 76% of people who search on smartphone for something nearby visit a business within 24 hours
- 28% of local searches result in a purchase

An optimized GBP can drive 30-50% of total organic traffic for local businesses.

---

## GBP Optimization Checklist (Complete)

### 1. Basic Information (Must-Complete)

#### Business Name
**Rule:** Use exact legal name. Do NOT stuff keywords.

**Correct:**
- "Green Valley Dental"
- "Maria's Hair Salon"

**Incorrect (will get suspended):**
- "Green Valley Dental | Best Dentist in Austin TX"
- "Maria's Hair Salon - Haircuts & Color Downtown"

**Exception:** If keywords are in your legal business name, that's fine. "Austin Emergency Plumbing" is okay if that's your registered DBA.

---

#### Categories

**Primary category:** Most important ranking factor. Be as specific as possible.

**Examples:**
- "Dentist" (generic)
- "Cosmetic Dentist" (specific — better)
- "Pediatric Dentist" (very specific — best)

**Secondary categories:** Add up to 9 additional categories. Choose variations of your core service.

**Strategy:**
- Primary = what you're best known for
- Secondary = related services you offer

**Example for multi-service business:**
- Primary: "General Contractor"
- Secondary: "Kitchen Remodeler", "Bathroom Remodeler", "Deck Builder", "Roofing Contractor"

**Common mistake:** Choosing categories you WANT to rank for instead of what you ACTUALLY do. Google uses AI to verify category alignment with your website/reviews. Misaligned categories hurt rankings.

---

#### Service Area vs. Physical Location

**Physical location (storefront):**
- Customers visit your business (restaurant, retail store, office)
- Address is publicly displayed
- Critical: Verify address via Google postcard

**Service area business (SAB):**
- You go to customers (plumber, landscaper, home cleaner)
- Address is hidden (only city shown)
- Define service radius (up to 200km) or specific cities

**Configuration:**
1. If SAB: Hide address in GBP settings
2. Define service area cities (be realistic — don't claim entire state)
3. Google penalizes fake addresses; use real business location even if hidden

---

#### Phone Number

**Best practices:**
- Use local number (not toll-free 1-800)
- Must match website footer/contact page (NAP consistency)
- Use tracking number ONLY if it forwards to real number (Google calls to verify)

**Why local numbers matter:** Search query "plumber near me" + local area code = ranking boost. Toll-free numbers don't signal geographic relevance.

---

#### Website URL

**Link to:**
- Homepage (if single location)
- Location-specific landing page (if multi-location)

**Example:** National chain with Austin location should link to `https://example.com/locations/austin` NOT `https://example.com`

**Tracking parameters:** Avoid `?utm_source=gbp` — clean URLs perform better. Use Google's built-in UTM tracking if needed.

---

#### Business Hours

**Complete every field:**
- Regular hours (Monday-Sunday)
- Special hours (holidays)
- "More hours" (pickup, delivery, drive-through, etc.)

**Why it matters:**
- 60% of searchers filter by "Open now"
- Incomplete hours = Google assumes you're closed = lost traffic

**Holiday hours:** Update 2 weeks before major holidays (Christmas, New Year, Thanksgiving). Google uses AI to predict hours; incorrect predictions hurt rankings.

---

#### Business Description

**Limits:**
- 750 characters max
- First 250 characters show in most placements (write front-loaded copy)

**Formula:**
```
[What you do] + [Who you serve] + [Geographic area] + [Differentiator] + [Keywords]
```

**Example (Dentist in São Paulo):**
```
Green Valley Dental offers comprehensive dental care for families in São Paulo's Pinheiros neighborhood. Our experienced dentists specialize in cosmetic dentistry, teeth whitening, implants, and emergency dental services. We accept all major insurance plans and offer same-day appointments. Serving São Paulo patients since 2010 with gentle, affordable dental care. Open evenings and Saturdays for your convenience.
```

**Keywords to include (naturally):**
- Primary service (dentist, plumber, lawyer)
- Geographic area (city, neighborhood)
- Service modifiers (emergency, affordable, experienced)
- Unique selling points (insurance accepted, same-day service)

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- Keyword stuffing ("best dentist, top dentist, #1 dentist")
- ALL CAPS
- Promotional language ("50% off")
- Links or HTML

---

### 2. Visual Content (Photos & Videos)

**Google's recommendation:** Upload at least 3 photos per week.

**Photo types:**

| Type | Specs | Best Practices |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **Logo** | 720×720px, square | Transparent background PNG; consistent with website |
| **Cover Photo** | 1024×576px, 16:9 | Storefront exterior, team photo, or hero image |
| **Interior** | 720×720px min | Well-lit, professional shots of space |
| **Exterior** | 720×720px min | Storefront with signage visible; take during daytime |
| **Team** | 720×720px min | Staff photos build trust; name tags visible |
| **Products** | 720×720px min | High-res, white background or in-use shots |
| **At Work** | 720×720px min | Service in action (dentist with patient, contractor on job site) |

**EXIF Geotagging:**
Photos with embedded GPS coordinates rank higher in local pack.

**How to geotag:**
1. Take photo with smartphone at business location (iPhone/Android auto-embed GPS)
2. Or use tool like GeoImgr (https://www.geoimgr.com/) to add coordinates
3. Upload to GBP

**Video:**
- 30 seconds max (shorter = higher completion rate)
- Show business in action (tour, service demo, team intro)
- Upload directly to GBP (not YouTube link)

**User-generated photos:** Encourage customers to post photos in reviews. Businesses with user photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks.

### 3. Products & Services

**Services section:**
Add every service you offer with detailed descriptions.

**Structure:**
- **Service name:** "Teeth Whitening"
- **Description:** "Professional in-office teeth whitening using Zoom technology. Results in 1 hour. Safe for sensitive teeth. $299 per session."
- **Price:** Optional but recommended (transparency builds trust)

**Why it matters:** Services show as rich results in search. User searches "teeth whitening são paulo" → your service listing appears in local pack.

**How many services:** Add 5-20. Too few = limited keyword coverage. Too many = looks spammy.

**Example (Plumber):**
- Emergency Plumbing Repair
- Drain Cleaning
- Water Heater Installation
- Leak Detection
- Bathroom Remodeling
- Sewer Line Repair

---

### 4. Attributes

Attributes are structured data fields Google extracts from your business.

**Categories:**
- **Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible, accessible parking
- **Amenities:** Free Wi-Fi, restroom, parking
- **Crowd:** LGBTQ+ friendly, family-friendly
- **Payments:** Credit cards, Apple Pay, cash only
- **Planning:** Reservations, appointments required
- **Service options:** Dine-in, takeout, delivery, online appointments

**Best practices:**
- Select ALL that apply (most businesses under-use attributes)
- Attributes are ranking signals — "wheelchair accessible" helps you rank for accessibility-related searches
- Verify attributes quarterly; Google AI auto-suggests based on user edits

---

### 5. Posts (GBP Updates)

GBP Posts are micro-content (like social media) that appear in your listing.

**Post types:**
- **What's New:** General updates ("New summer menu available")
- **Event:** Upcoming event with date/time
- **Offer:** Promotion with coupon code or discount
- **Product:** Highlight specific product with photo + price

**Best practices:**
- Post 1-2× per week (keeps listing fresh)
- Add photo (posts with images get 40% more clicks)
- Include CTA button ("Book Now", "Learn More", "Sign Up")
- Posts expire after 7 days (Event posts expire on event date)

**SEO benefit:** Posts don't directly affect rankings, but increase engagement (clicks, calls, direction requests) which IS a ranking factor.

---

### 6. Questions & Answers

Users can ask questions on your GBP. Unanswered questions = lost conversions.

**Strategy:**
1. **Seed your own Q&A:** Post FAQs and answer them yourself.
   - "Do you accept insurance?" → "Yes, we accept all major dental insurance plans."
   - "Do you offer same-day appointments?" → "Yes, call us before 2pm for same-day availability."
2. **Monitor weekly:** Respond to new questions within 24 hours.
3. **Upvote helpful answers:** Other users can answer; upvote correct ones, downvote spam.

**Common questions to pre-answer:**
- Payment methods accepted
- Parking availability
- Hours on holidays
- Booking process
- Service area coverage

## Local Schema Markup

Schema markup is structured data (JSON-LD) that tells Google exactly what your business is, what you do, and where you operate.

### LocalBusiness Schema (Required)

**Base template:**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Green Valley Dental",
  "image": "https://example.com/logo.png",
  "telephone": "+1-512-555-0100",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street",
    "addressLocality": "Austin",
    "addressRegion": "TX",
    "postalCode": "78701",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": "30.2672",
    "longitude": "-97.7431"
  },
  "url": "https://greenvalleydental.com",
  "openingHoursSpecification": [
    {
      "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification",
      "dayOfWeek": ["Monday", "Tuesday", "Wednesday", "Thursday", "Friday"],
      "opens": "08:00",
      "closes": "17:00"
    }
  ],
  "priceRange": "$$"
}
```

**Specific business types (use instead of generic LocalBusiness):**
- **Dentist**, **Restaurant**, **Store**, **Attorney**, **RealEstateAgent**, **Electrician**, **Plumber**, **HairSalon**, **AutoRepair**

**Example for Dentist:**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Dentist",
  "name": "Green Valley Dental",
  ...
}
```

**Advanced properties:**
- `priceRange`: "$", "$$", "$$$", "$$$$"
- `paymentAccepted`: "Cash, Credit Card, Insurance"
- `currenciesAccepted`: "USD", "BRL"
- `areaServed`: "Austin, TX" or ["Austin", "Round Rock", "Cedar Park"]

---

### Multi-Location Schema

If you have multiple locations, use **@id** to create unique entities:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Dentist",
  "@id": "https://example.com/locations/austin",
  "name": "Green Valley Dental - Austin",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main St",
    "addressLocality": "Austin",
    "addressRegion": "TX",
    "postalCode": "78701"
  }
}
```

**Each location needs:**
- Unique `@id` (URL of location page)
- Unique `name` (include location in name)
- Unique `address`

---

### FAQ Schema (High-Value)

FAQ schema creates rich snippets in search results (expandable Q&A boxes).

**Template:**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Do you accept dental insurance?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Yes, we accept all major dental insurance plans including Aetna, Cigna, Delta Dental, and MetLife. We also offer affordable payment plans for uninsured patients."
      }
    },
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "Do you offer emergency dental services?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Yes, we provide same-day emergency dental care for toothaches, broken teeth, and dental injuries. Call us at 512-555-0100 for immediate assistance."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**Best practices:**
- Add 3-10 FAQs per page
- Answer questions users actually ask (check Google Search Console "queries" for ideas)
- Keep answers under 300 characters for snippet optimization

## NAP Consistency (Critical)

**NAP = Name, Address, Phone**

Google's algorithm matches NAP across the web to verify your business is legitimate. Inconsistencies = trust penalty = lower rankings.

### NAP Audit Checklist

Check these locations for 100% identical NAP:

**Primary:**
- [ ] Google Business Profile
- [ ] Website footer
- [ ] Website contact page
- [ ] Apple Maps (https://mapsconnect.apple.com)
- [ ] Bing Places (https://www.bingplaces.com)

**Directories:**
- [ ] Yelp
- [ ] Facebook Business Page
- [ ] LinkedIn Company Page
- [ ] Yellow Pages
- [ ] Industry-specific directories (Avvo for lawyers, Healthgrades for doctors, etc.)

**Social Media:**
- [ ] Instagram bio
- [ ] Twitter bio
- [ ] YouTube About

---

### Common NAP Inconsistencies

| Issue | Example | Fix |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Abbreviations** | "123 Main St" vs "123 Main Street" | Standardize (use full "Street" everywhere) |
| **Suite numbers** | "123 Main St" vs "123 Main St Suite 200" | Include suite consistently |
| **Phone formatting** | "(512) 555-0100" vs "512-555-0100" | Standardize format (either is fine, just pick one) |
| **Business name** | "Green Valley Dental" vs "Green Valley Dental Clinic" | Use exact legal name everywhere |

**Tools to find NAP inconsistencies:**
- **Moz Local** (paid, $99/year)
- **Yext** (paid, starts $199/year)
- **Manual Google search:** Search `"your business name" "your city"` and check all results

**Goal:** 50-100 high-quality citations. Diminishing returns after that.

## Review Management

**Reviews are the #2 local ranking factor** (after GBP optimization).

**Statistics:**
- 98% of consumers read online reviews for local businesses
- 1-star increase = 5-9% revenue increase
- Businesses with 50+ reviews are seen as trustworthy

### Review Volume Strategy

**Goal:** 5+ reviews per month minimum.

**Review request workflow:**
1. **Identify happy customers:** Post-purchase survey, post-service email, or direct ask
2. **Make it easy:** Send direct GBP review link (not just "leave us a review")
3. **Timing:** Ask within 24-48 hours of positive interaction (strike while iron is hot)

**GBP review link format:**
```
https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID
```

Find your `placeid`: https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/place-id

**Automate review requests:**
- **Email automation:** Send review request 3 days after purchase/service
- **SMS:** Higher open rate (98% vs 20% email)
- **In-person ask:** Train staff to request reviews from satisfied customers

**Tools:**
- **BirdEye** (review request automation)
- **Podium** (SMS-based review requests)
- **Grade.us** (review management platform)

---

### Review Quality (Star Rating + Keywords)

**Star rating:** 4.0+ is minimum for local pack inclusion. 4.5+ is competitive.

**Keywords in reviews:** Google reads review text for ranking signals.

**Example:**
- **Good review:** "Best dentist in Austin! Dr. Smith did my teeth whitening and it looks amazing."
- **Better review:** "Best cosmetic dentist in Austin! Dr. Smith did my professional teeth whitening and the results are incredible. Highly recommend for anyone looking for affordable teeth whitening in Austin."

**How to get keyword-rich reviews:**
- Ask specific questions: "How was your teeth whitening experience?"
- Not: "Leave us a review" (too vague)

**Avoid:** Asking for keyword-stuffed reviews = violation of Google's review policies. Let users naturally mention services.

## Hreflang Implementation (Detailed)

### When to Use Hreflang

**Use hreflang if:**
- You have multiple language versions of same content
- You serve different countries with localized content
- You have country-specific TLDs (.com, .com.br, .co.uk)

**Don't use if:**
- Single language, single country site
- Content is not translated (hreflang is for equivalent pages)

---

### Hreflang Syntax

**Format:**
```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="[language]-[region]" href="[URL]" />
```

**Language codes:** ISO 639-1 (en, pt, es, fr, de)
**Region codes:** ISO 3166-1 Alpha 2 (US, BR, GB, MX)

**Examples:**
- `en-US` = English, United States
- `en-GB` = English, United Kingdom
- `pt-BR` = Portuguese, Brazil
- `pt-PT` = Portuguese, Portugal
- `es-MX` = Spanish, Mexico
- `es-ES` = Spanish, Spain

**Language-only (no region):**
- `en` = English (any region)
- `pt` = Portuguese (any region)

**Use language-only when content is not region-specific.**

---

### Implementation Methods

#### 1. HTML `<link>` Tags (Recommended)

Add to `<head>` section of each page:

```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-US" href="https://example.com/en/page" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="pt-BR" href="https://example.com/pt-br/page" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="es-MX" href="https://example.com/es/page" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://example.com/" />
```

**Rules:**
- Every page must reference itself + all language versions
- Must be bidirectional (EN page links to PT-BR, PT-BR links back)
- Include `x-default` for fallback (users with unmatched language)

---

#### 2. XML Sitemap (Alternative)

Add hreflang to sitemap instead of HTML:

```xml
<url>
  <loc>https://example.com/en/page</loc>
  <xhtml:link 
    rel="alternate" 
    hreflang="pt-BR" 
    href="https://example.com/pt-br/page" />
  <xhtml:link 
    rel="alternate" 
    hreflang="en-US" 
    href="https://example.com/en/page" />
  <xhtml:link 
    rel="alternate" 
    hreflang="x-default" 
    href="https://example.com/" />
</url>
```

**Use when:** Large site with 1000+ pages (sitemap is easier to maintain than HTML tags).

---

#### 3. HTTP Headers (Rare)

For non-HTML files (PDFs, etc.):

```
Link: <https://example.com/en/file.pdf>; rel="alternate"; hreflang="en",
      <https://example.com/pt-br/file.pdf>; rel="alternate"; hreflang="pt-BR"
```

---

### Common Hreflang Mistakes

1. **Non-reciprocal links:** EN page links to PT-BR, but PT-BR doesn't link back to EN.
   - **Fix:** Ensure all pages reference all versions (including themselves).

2. **Wrong language codes:** Using `en-UK` instead of `en-GB`, or `pt` instead of `pt-BR`.
   - **Fix:** Use correct ISO codes (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ISO_639-1_codes).

3. **Missing self-referential tag:** EN page must include `hreflang="en-US" href="[itself]"`.
   - **Fix:** Always include self-reference.

4. **Forgetting x-default:** No fallback for unmatched languages.
   - **Fix:** Add `x-default` pointing to default language version.

5. **Relative URLs:** `hreflang="/pt-br/page"` instead of `https://example.com/pt-br/page`.
   - **Fix:** Always use absolute URLs.

---

1. Navigate to "International Targeting" → "Language"

## Local SEO Audit Workflow

### 1. GBP Audit (30 min)

- [ ] All fields 100% complete
- [ ] Primary + 5 secondary categories selected
- [ ] 10+ geotagged photos uploaded
- [ ] 5+ services added with descriptions
- [ ] Business hours accurate (including holidays)
- [ ] Posts published in last 7 days
- [ ] Q&A section has 5+ pre-answered questions
- [ ] Respond to all reviews (last 30 days)

---

### 2. NAP Audit (60 min)

- [ ] NAP matches exactly across GBP, website, Apple Maps, Bing
- [ ] Check top 20 Google results for `"[business name]" "[city]"` for inconsistencies
- [ ] Verify NAP on Yelp, Facebook, LinkedIn
- [ ] Fix any abbreviation mismatches (St vs Street)

---

## Advanced Local SEO Topics

### Local Link Building

**Goal:** Get backlinks from local websites to boost geographic relevance.

**Strategies:**
1. **Local sponsorships:** Sponsor Little League team, charity event → get link from event page
2. **Local news:** Pitch story to local newspaper/blog → earn editorial link
3. **Chamber of Commerce:** Join local business association → directory link
4. **Local suppliers:** Ask vendors to list you as customer → link from their site
5. **Local partnerships:** Cross-promote with complementary businesses → reciprocal links

**Quality > quantity:** 1 link from local newspaper > 10 links from random directories.

---

### Google Posts SEO

Google Posts don't directly affect rankings, but increase CTR (click-through rate), which IS a ranking signal.

**Post types:**
- **Weekly update:** "New service available", "Blog post published"
- **Monthly offer:** "10% off this month" (with promo code)
- **Quarterly event:** "Open house", "Grand reopening"

**Best practices:**
- 100-300 words (long enough to be useful, short enough to read quickly)
- Add CTA button (Book Now, Learn More, Call Now)
- Include photo (1200×900px recommended)
- Post Monday-Friday 9am-12pm (highest engagement window)

---

### Voice Search Optimization

**Voice searches are 3x more likely to be local** ("near me" queries).

**Optimization:**
- Target question keywords ("where is", "how do I", "what's the best")
- Answer in FAQ format (voice assistants pull from FAQ schema)
- Use conversational language (how people speak, not type)
- Optimize for featured snippets (position 0 = voice search answer)

**Example:**
- **Typed query:** "dentist austin tx"
- **Voice query:** "Where's the best dentist near me?"

**Optimization:** Add FAQ "What's the best dentist in Austin?" with answer highlighting your services.

### Local Inventory Ads (Retail)

If you sell products in-store, enable **Local Inventory Ads** (LIA).

**How it works:**
1. Upload product inventory feed to Google Merchant Center
2. Google shows "In stock nearby" for local searches
3. Users click → see store location + directions

**Requirements:**
- Google Merchant Center account
- Product feed with inventory levels
- GBP linked to Merchant Center

**Use case:** Retail stores with physical inventory (electronics, apparel, home goods).
