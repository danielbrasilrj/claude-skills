## 1. Copywriting Frameworks

### PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)

**Structure:**
1. **Problem** — Identify the pain point your audience experiences
2. **Agitate** — Amplify the emotional impact of that problem
3. **Solution** — Present your product as the resolution

**When to use:**
- Short-form copy (ads, push notifications, social posts)
- Pain-point-driven products (productivity tools, health apps)
- Audience is already problem-aware

**Example:**
```
Problem: "Spending hours on manual data entry?"
Agitate: "Every minute wasted is money lost. Your team could be closing deals instead."
Solution: "Automate your workflow in 5 minutes with DataSync."
```

**Best practices:**
- Problem: Be specific. "Tired of X" beats "Having trouble"
- Agitate: Use sensory details and consequences
- Solution: Link directly to the agitated problem

---

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)

**Structure:**
1. **Attention** — Hook with a bold claim, question, or stat
2. **Interest** — Elaborate on the benefit; explain "why this matters"
3. **Desire** — Build emotional connection; show social proof
4. **Action** — Clear CTA with urgency or incentive

**When to use:**
- Long-form copy (landing pages, app store descriptions)
- Structured sales flow needed
- Audience needs education before converting

**Example:**
```
Attention: "Book appointments 10x faster"
Interest: "Our AI scheduler syncs calendars, sends reminders, and reschedules conflicts — all automatically."
Desire: "Join 50,000+ teams who've reclaimed 15 hours per week."
Action: "Start your 14-day free trial — no credit card required."
```

**Best practices:**
- Attention: Lead with the strongest benefit, not the product name
- Interest: Answer "so what?" for every claim
- Desire: Use numbers, testimonials, or case studies
- Action: Single clear CTA; avoid choice paralysis

---

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)

**Structure:**
1. **Before** — Describe the current frustrating state
2. **After** — Paint a vivid picture of the improved state
3. **Bridge** — Show how your product bridges the gap

**When to use:**
- Testimonials and case studies
- Transformation-focused products (fitness, productivity)
- Visual before/after scenarios

**Example:**
```
Before: "Sarah spent 3 hours every Monday manually creating reports."
After: "Now she clicks one button and reports generate in 30 seconds."
Bridge: "ReportBot automated her entire workflow in under 10 minutes."
```

---

### 4Ps (Promise → Picture → Proof → Push)

**Structure:**
1. **Promise** — Make a bold, specific claim
2. **Picture** — Help them visualize the outcome
3. **Proof** — Back it up with data, testimonials, or authority
4. **Push** — Drive action with urgency or incentive

**When to use:**
- High-stakes offers (expensive products, long commitments)
- Skeptical audiences who need proof
- Sales pages and webinar funnels

**Example:**
```
Promise: "Cut your AWS costs by 40% in 30 days."
Picture: "Imagine $4,000 back in your budget every month, with zero downtime."
Proof: "We've saved 2,000+ companies $50M+ combined. Featured in TechCrunch."
Push: "Book your free audit today — 20 spots left this month."
```

## 2. CTA Optimization

### CTA Anatomy

Every CTA needs:
1. **Action verb** — Start with "Get," "Start," "Claim," "Join," "Try"
2. **Value proposition** — What they get (not what they do)
3. **Friction reducer** — "Free," "No credit card," "Cancel anytime"

### High-Converting CTA Patterns

| Pattern | Example | When to Use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Benefit-driven** | "Get My Free Guide" | Lead magnets, downloads |
| **Time-bound** | "Start Your 14-Day Trial" | Free trials, limited offers |
| **Social proof** | "Join 10,000+ Users" | Community-driven products |
| **Urgency** | "Claim Your Spot (5 Left)" | Scarcity-based offers |
| **Curiosity** | "See How It Works" | Demo videos, product tours |
| **Zero-risk** | "Try Free — No Credit Card" | High-friction signups |

### CTA Testing Checklist

- [ ] Uses action verb (not "Submit" or "Click here")
- [ ] Specifies value ("Get," not "Learn more")
- [ ] Reduces friction (free, instant, no commitment)
- [ ] Stands out visually (color contrast, whitespace)
- [ ] Single CTA per section (avoid choice paralysis)
- [ ] Mobile thumb-friendly (min 44x44px tap target)

## 3. A/B Test Copy Variants

### What to Test

| Element | Variants | Impact |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Headlines** | Benefit vs curiosity vs question | High |
| **CTA text** | "Start free" vs "Get started" vs "Try now" | High |
| **Social proof** | Number vs testimonial vs logo wall | Medium |
| **Copy length** | Short vs long-form | Medium |
| **Urgency** | With deadline vs without | Medium |
| **Tone** | Formal vs casual | Low |

### Testing Framework

1. **Hypothesis:** "Changing X will increase Y because Z"
2. **Variants:** A (control) vs B (treatment)
3. **Metrics:** Primary (conversion rate) + Secondary (bounce rate, time on page)
4. **Sample size:** Minimum 100 conversions per variant
5. **Duration:** Run until statistical significance (usually 1-2 weeks)

### Variant Examples

**Headline Test:**
- A: "Book Appointments Online" (feature-focused)
- B: "Never Miss a Client Again" (benefit-focused)
- C: "Are You Losing Clients to No-Shows?" (question)

**CTA Test:**
- A: "Sign Up Free"
- B: "Start Your Free Trial"
- C: "Get Started — It's Free"

## 4. Headline Formulas

### 1. Number + Adjective + Keyword + Promise
"7 Proven Ways to Double Your Email Open Rates"

### 2. How to + Desired Outcome + Time Frame
"How to Write Converting Copy in 30 Minutes"

### 3. Question + Pain Point
"Struggling to Get Customers to Click?"

### 4. Before/After Transformation
"From 2% to 12% Conversion in 60 Days"

### 5. Negative Angle
"Stop Wasting Money on Ads That Don't Convert"

### 6. Curiosity Gap
"The Secret to Landing Pages That Sell (Hint: It's Not Design)"

### 7. Testimonial-Style
"How Sarah Tripled Her Revenue with One Headline Change"

### 8. Specificity + Proof
"1,247 Customers Switched in March — Here's Why"

### Headline Testing Checklist

- [ ] Includes a number or specific metric
- [ ] Addresses a clear pain point or desire
- [ ] Under 70 characters (mobile-friendly)
- [ ] Passes the "so what?" test
- [ ] Uses power words (proven, secret, effortless, instant)
- [ ] Avoids jargon and buzzwords
- [ ] Creates curiosity without being clickbait

## 5. Brazilian Portuguese Copy Adaptation

### Transcreation vs Translation

**Translation** (literal):
- "Sign up now" → "Cadastre-se agora"

**Transcreation** (adapted):
- "Sign up now" → "Comece grátis" ("Start for free" — more compelling in pt-BR context)

### Cultural Adaptation Rules

#### 1. Formality Level
- **Use "você"** (informal, friendly) — not "o senhor/a senhora" (formal)
- Exception: Legal, financial, or government contexts use formal tone

#### 2. Emotional Triggers
Brazilian audiences respond strongly to:
- **Community/belonging** — "Junte-se a milhares de pessoas" (Join thousands)
- **Status/exclusivity** — "Acesso VIP" (VIP access)
- **Family/care** — "Cuide de quem você ama" (Care for those you love)

#### 3. Call-to-Action Patterns

| English | Direct Translation | Better pt-BR Adaptation |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Get started | Comece | Começar agora / Quero começar |
| Try free | Experimente grátis | Testar grátis / Experimente sem compromisso |
| Learn more | Saiba mais | Ver como funciona / Conhecer melhor |
| Buy now | Compre agora | Quero comprar / Garantir meu desconto |
| Sign up | Cadastre-se | Criar conta grátis / Começar grátis |

**Pattern:** pt-BR prefers "I want to" (Quero) over imperative commands

#### 4. Text Expansion Guidelines

| English Length | Expansion Factor | Example |
|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| 1-10 chars | +30% | "Save" (4) → "Salvar" (6) |
| 11-20 chars | +25% | "Get started" (11) → "Começar agora" (13) |
| 21-40 chars | +20% | "Book your free trial" (20) → "Comece seu teste grátis" (25) |
| 40+ chars | +15% | Full paragraphs |

**Design implications:**
- Never use fixed-width containers for buttons
- Allow 2 lines for single-line labels
- Test UI with longest pt-BR strings

#### 5. Number and Currency Formatting

| Type | English | pt-BR |
|------|---------|-------|
| Decimal | 1,234.56 | 1.234,56 |
| Currency | $49.99 | R$ 49,99 |
| Dates | 03/15/2025 | 15/03/2025 |
| Time | 3:45 PM | 15h45 |
| Percentages | 25% off | 25% de desconto |

#### 6. Urgency and Scarcity

**English:** "Only 5 spots left"
**pt-BR:** "Restam apenas 5 vagas" OR "Últimas vagas disponíveis"

**English:** "Offer ends tonight"
**pt-BR:** "Oferta termina hoje à meia-noite" OR "Última chance — termina hoje"

Brazilian audiences respond well to:
- Time-based urgency (deadlines)
- Quantity scarcity (limited spots)
- Social proof urgency ("100 pessoas compraram hoje")

#### 7. Social Proof Adaptation

**English:** "Trusted by 10,000+ customers"
**pt-BR:** "Mais de 10 mil clientes confiam" OR "Escolhido por 10 mil pessoas"

**Pattern:** pt-BR uses "mais de" (more than) instead of "10,000+"

#### 8. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use Instead | Why |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Literal English structure | Natural pt-BR flow | Sounds robotic |
| "Grátis grátis grátis" | "Comece sem pagar nada" | Overuse reduces trust |
| Overly formal tone | Conversational "você" | Feels cold/distant |
| American idioms | Local expressions | Lost in translation |
| Generic benefits | Specific outcomes | Low engagement |

#### 9. Power Words in pt-BR

| Category | Words |
|----------|-------|
| Urgency | Agora, hoje, última chance, restam apenas |
| Value | Grátis, sem custo, bônus, desconto exclusivo |
| Ease | Fácil, simples, rápido, em minutos |
| Proof | Comprovado, testado, garantido, aprovado |
| Exclusivity | Exclusivo, VIP, acesso especial, membros apenas |

#### 10. Email Subject Line Adaptations

**English:** "Your free trial ends tomorrow"
**pt-BR:** "Seu teste grátis termina amanhã ⏰"

**English:** "50% off — today only"
**pt-BR:** "50% OFF — só hoje! 🔥"

**Notes:**
- pt-BR uses more emojis in subject lines (higher open rates)
- Prefers "você" (your) for personalization
- "OFF" is commonly used untranslated for discounts

## Copy Review Checklist

### Before Publishing Any Copy:

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Passes "5-year-old test" — simple, jargon-free
- [ ] One idea per sentence
- [ ] Active voice, second person ("you")

**Conversion:**
- [ ] Leads with strongest benefit
- [ ] Includes specific numbers/proof
- [ ] Single clear CTA
- [ ] Reduces friction (free, instant, risk-free)

**Localization (pt-BR):**
- [ ] Transcreated, not translated
- [ ] Text expansion accounted for (+20-30%)
- [ ] Numbers, dates, currency formatted correctly
- [ ] Uses "você" tone, not formal

**Testing:**
- [ ] Hypothesis documented for A/B test
- [ ] Variants test one variable only
- [ ] Minimum sample size planned (100+ conversions/variant)
